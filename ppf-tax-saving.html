PPF & tax-saving basics

A plain overview of Public Provident Fund (PPF) and common Section 80C-style options in India. Not tax advice. Rules change; confirm with official sources or a qualified professional.

What is PPF?

Public Provident Fund (PPF) is a long-term savings scheme backed by the Government of India. You can open an account at a participating bank or post office and deposit money each year (within notified limits).

Interest rates are set by the government and can change over time. People usually talk about PPF for long-horizon, relatively stable compounding, not for short-term trading or high growth.

Features people usually care about

  • 📅Long lock-in: Classic PPF has a multi-year tenure (commonly discussed as 15 years), with extension options under the rules.
  • 📅Deposit band: Minimum and maximum yearly deposits are set by the scheme. Many guides cite ₹500 minimum and ₹1.5 lakh maximum; always verify current limits.
  • 📈Interest: Credited as per scheme rules. The rate is not the same as equity market returns.
  • 🏠Partial withdrawals / loans: Allowed only under specific conditions and timelines.
  • 🧾Tax treatment: Historically linked with EEE-style treatment for eligible contributions, interest, and maturity under then-current law. Always re-check current tax law.
Try numbers: Use our simplified PPF calculator to see how annual deposits might grow under an assumed rate. It is an educational model, not an official quote.

Who often looks at PPF?

  • 📅People who want a long-term, relatively predictable savings bucket
  • 📈People already using equity SIPs who want a debt-like core
  • 🧾Anyone exploring options that often come up in tax-saving talks

PPF may fit poorly if you need money soon, want high market-linked growth, or want full liquidity.

Section 80C options (very high level)

Indian tax law has historically allowed deductions up to a combined limit (often discussed as ₹1.5 lakh under Section 80C and related clauses) for eligible investments and expenses. Names that often come up include:

  • 🏦PPF contributions
  • 📅ELSS mutual funds (market-linked; lock-in typically 3 years)
  • 🏠Life insurance premiums (depends on the product)
  • 🏦EPF, certain deposits, tuition fees, principal repayment on home loans (context-specific)
  • 📈NPS has additional clauses (for example talks around 80CCD) beyond plain 80C
Important: Eligibility, limits, and tax treatment depend on your status, the financial year, and current law. This site does not prepare returns or certify deductions. Use official Income Tax materials or a qualified tax professional.

Practical cautions

  • 🧾Do not invest only for a tax deduction. Cash flow and goals come first.
  • 🔒Do not lock money you may need for emergencies into long lock-ins.
  • 🔀Compare costs and lock-ins before buying insurance-linked “investment” products.
  • 📁Keep proof of deposits and account statements each year.
