ETFs guide

Exchange-traded funds package an index or asset class into units you can buy like stocks. This guide covers types, costs, how ETFs differ from index mutual funds, and a sample SIP illustration for learning.

What is an ETF?

An exchange-traded fund (ETF) holds a basket of securities (or commodities) and trades on the exchange during market hours at a live price. Most equity ETFs aim to track an index (for example the Nifty 50) at low cost. You get mutual-fund-style diversification with stock-like trading.

Strengths: Diversification in one ticker, usually low expense ratios, clear holdings, and real-time quotes.
Caveats: You need a Demat account. Bid–ask spreads and tracking difference matter. Market risk of the underlying still applies.

How to invest in ETFs

  1. 🪪Complete KYC and open a Demat and trading account with a SEBI-registered broker.
  2. 🔎Search the ticker (broad Nifty ETFs are often discussed as NIFTYBEES-style products).
  3. 📉Prefer limit orders near fair value. Check AUM, expense ratio, and tracking difference.
  4. 💼Hold units in Demat and sell during market hours like a stock.
  5. 🔁For automatic SIPs, many people use index mutual funds instead. Compare both below.

Costs & tracking (checklist)

  • 💸Expense ratio: ongoing fee built into the NAV
  • 📉Tracking difference: how much the ETF lags (or leads) its index over time
  • ↔️Bid–ask spread: trading cost on the exchange
  • 💧Liquidity / AUM: thin ETFs can be costly to enter and exit
  • 🧾Tax: equity vs non-equity classification matters, and rules change. See the tax overview

ETF SIP returns – FAQs

What is SIP in ETFs?
SIP means investing a fixed amount regularly (for example monthly) into an ETF, much like in mutual funds. In practice you buy ETF units each month through your broker and build a holding over time.
What is XIRR and why is it important for SIP?
XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return) is the annualised return for cash flows on different dates. For SIPs it better reflects your real return because it accounts for when each instalment was invested. Plain CAGR does not.
Why is ETF SIP XIRR different from index return?
Index returns usually assume a lump sum at the start. SIP XIRR reflects that you buy at different prices (sometimes high, sometimes low), which is the “rupee cost averaging” effect. SIP XIRR can sit above or below index CAGR depending on how the market moved.
Can I do SIP in ETFs automatically?
Most brokers in India still do not offer automatic SIP for ETFs the way mutual funds do. You usually buy units manually each month. Some discount brokers offer buy reminders or API-based auto-invest.
Why does the final corpus differ between ETFs?
Each ETF has its own price path and volatility. Equity ETFs like NIFTYBEES may beat gold ETFs like GOLDBEES over long stretches, but short windows can look very different. The gold run in this sample is an illustration, not a forecast.
Is there any tax on ETF SIP returns?
Tax depends on whether the ETF is treated as equity-oriented under current law, how long you hold, and the Finance Act for that year. Rules change. See our tax overview and official Income Tax sources. This is not tax advice.
Is XIRR the best metric to track my real SIP performance?
XIRR is the standard way to measure returns on SIPs or any staggered investment, because it adjusts for both timing and amount of cash flows.

Who often uses ETFs?

  • 💸Investors with Demat accounts who want low-cost index exposure
  • 💧People comfortable placing exchange orders (or limit orders)
  • 🎯Anyone building a passive core next to SIPs in mutual funds
Tip: Want auto-debit SIPs with no Demat? Index mutual funds often feel simpler. Want exchange liquidity and very low costs? ETFs may suit you. Compare total costs, not just TER.

Common mistakes

  • 🔎Chasing the lowest TER alone while ignoring tracking difference and spreads
  • 🎯Treating thin sector ETFs as a diversified core
  • ⚡Day-trading ETFs without a plan
  • 📜Assuming tax rules never change. Re-check equity vs non-equity classification.

FAQs

Are ETFs “safe”?
SEBI regulation does not remove market risk of the underlying. Safety is not the same as guaranteed returns.
Can I SIP an ETF?
Many brokers lack automatic SIP. Investors often buy monthly by hand or use index mutual funds when they want automation.
Minimum investment?
Usually one unit. The cost depends on the ETF’s market quote.
