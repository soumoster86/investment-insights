What is an ETF?

ETF stands for Exchange Traded Fund. It is a basket of securities—such as stocks, bonds, or commodities—that is traded on an exchange, just like a stock. ETFs combine the diversification benefits of mutual funds with the ease of trading like stocks. They are designed to track an index (like Nifty 50, Sensex), a sector, or a commodity.

Key Benefits of ETFs

  • Diversification: Get exposure to a wide range of securities in a single trade.
  • Low Cost: Lower expense ratios compared to actively managed funds.
  • Liquidity: Buy or sell ETFs anytime during market hours at real-time prices.
  • Transparency: Holdings are disclosed daily, so you know exactly what you own.
  • Tax Efficiency: Fewer capital gains distributions compared to mutual funds.

Types of ETFs in India

  • Equity ETFs: Track stock indices like Nifty 50, Sensex, Nifty Next 50, sectoral indices (Banking, IT, Pharma, etc.).
  • Debt/Bond ETFs: Invest in government or corporate bonds (e.g., Bharat Bond ETF).
  • Gold ETFs: Track the price of gold, a popular way to invest in the yellow metal digitally.
  • International ETFs: Track overseas indices like Nasdaq 100, S&P 500, etc.
  • Sector/Thematic ETFs: Focus on specific sectors like PSU Banks, IT, Infra, etc.

How to Invest in ETFs

  1. Open a Demat and trading account with a broker (Zerodha, Groww, ICICI Direct, etc.).
  2. Search for the desired ETF by its ticker (e.g., NIFTYBEES for Nifty 50 ETF, BANKBEES for Nifty Bank ETF).
  3. Place a buy order during market hours, similar to buying shares.
  4. ETFs can be held in your Demat account and sold anytime on the exchange.

ETF SIP Returns Calculator – FAQs

What is SIP in ETFs?
SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a disciplined way of investing a fixed amount regularly (e.g., monthly) into an ETF, just like in mutual funds. In practice, you buy ETF units each month via your broker, accumulating units over time.
What is XIRR and why is it important for SIP?
XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return) gives you the annualized return for a series of cash flows occurring at different dates. For SIPs, it accurately reflects your real return by considering each investment’s timing, which plain CAGR does not.
Why is ETF SIP XIRR different from index return?
Index returns are based on a lump-sum investment at the start. SIP XIRR considers that you buy at different prices (sometimes high, sometimes low) and thus get a “rupee cost averaging” benefit. The SIP XIRR can be lower or higher than index CAGR, depending on market trends.
Can I do SIP in ETFs automatically?
Most brokers in India do not yet offer automated SIP for ETFs (unlike mutual funds). You need to manually buy ETF units each month, but some discount brokers allow scheduled “buy reminders” or auto-invest via APIs.
Why does the final corpus differ between ETFs?
The difference comes from each ETF’s price growth and volatility. Equity ETFs like NIFTYBEES may outperform gold ETFs like GOLDBEES over long periods, but short-term returns may vary.
Is there any tax on ETF SIP returns?
Yes. Like stocks, you pay capital gains tax when selling ETF units: Equity ETFs (NIFTYBEES) attract 15% STCG (<1 year) and 10% LTCG (>1 year, above ₹1L). Gold and Bond ETFs are taxed as non-equity (20% with indexation after 3 years).
Is XIRR the best metric to track my real SIP performance?
Yes! XIRR is the industry-standard method to measure actual investor returns for SIP or any staggered investment, as it adjusts for both timing and amount of cash flows.

Who Should Invest in ETFs?

  • Investors comfortable using a Demat/trading account.
  • Those seeking low-cost, diversified, and passive investing.
  • Investors who want intraday liquidity and control over buy/sell price.
  • Long-term investors seeking index-based growth or asset class exposure (e.g., gold, bonds).

Tip: If you want a simple, SIP-based approach, consider index mutual funds. If you want intraday trading and lowest cost, go for ETFs.

FAQs about ETFs

Are ETFs safe?
ETFs are regulated by SEBI in India and are as safe as the underlying assets (stocks, bonds, etc.). Market risk applies.
Do ETFs pay dividends?
Some ETFs pay out dividends; others are growth-oriented and reinvest dividends back into the fund.
Is there any minimum investment?
Usually, you can buy as little as 1 unit of an ETF, which can be just ₹100–₹200 for some funds.
Can NRIs invest in Indian ETFs?
Yes, most Indian ETFs are open to NRIs subject to regulations.
