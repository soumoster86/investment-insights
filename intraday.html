Intraday trading overview

Same-day trading concepts, leverage risk, and basic terms. Most beginners lose money with leverage. This page is not a trading system.

What is intraday trading?

Intraday trading means opening and closing positions in the same session (for example, equity MIS products). The goal is short-term price moves, not multi-year ownership. Brokers square off open intraday positions before close.

Why people try it: A chance at quick gains, access to margin products, and full use of the trading day.
Why it is hard: Costs, taxes, and leverage can wipe out a small edge. Emotions run high, and brokers force square-off near close.

How it works

  1. 🏷️Choose an intraday product (often labelled MIS / intraday) on your broker.
  2. ⚡Trade with margin. Leverage amplifies both gains and losses.
  3. ⏰Close the position yourself, or the broker will square it off near market close.
  4. 📋P&L settles for the day (plus charges). Overnight investing risk is different from MIS.

Glossary

  • 🔗Margin: Money borrowed from the broker so you can trade larger positions than your cash balance alone.
  • 💧Square off: Closing an open position (buy or sell) within the same trading day.
  • ⚠️Stop-loss: An order that closes a position at a preset price to limit loss.
  • 🛟Volume: Number of shares traded in a period. High volume usually means better liquidity.
  • ⚠️Volatility: How much the price swings. Higher volatility means larger moves.
  • 📅Leverage: Using borrowed money to raise potential return and risk.
  • 📈Order book: Live list of buy and sell orders for a stock.
  • 💸Bid-ask spread: Gap between the highest buy price and the lowest sell price.
  • 📈Intraday high/low: Highest and lowest price of a stock during the day.
  • 💡Scalping: Many small trades aiming for small gains.

Strategy names (concepts only)

Labels traders use. None are guaranteed edges after costs.

  • 🛡️Momentum: Follow strong short-term moves. Exit when the move fades.
  • 📌Breakout: Enter when price breaks a range with volume. False breaks are common.
  • 💡Mean reversion: Bet that extremes snap back. This can fail in strong trends.
  • 💸Scalping: Many tiny trades. Fees and speed dominate the result.

Risk management tips

  • 💰Only risk money you can lose without harming goals or rent.
  • 🛑Set a stop-loss before you enter. Avoid revenge trading.
  • 💧Prefer liquid names. Watch total costs (brokerage, STT, GST, and so on).
  • 📓Keep a trade journal. If results stay negative after costs, stop.
  • 🌱Long-term wealth usually comes from investing, not day trading. See the Stocks guide.

Common mistakes

  • 🛑No stop-loss and oversized leverage
  • 🔄Overtrading or chasing losses after a red day
  • 🧾Ignoring costs and taxes
  • 🪙Trading illiquid or penny stocks
  • 📣Treating tips as research

FAQs

Is intraday suitable for beginners?
Usually a poor first step. Most people should learn the basics and long-term investing first. Paper trade if you must experiment.
What is the minimum amount needed for intraday trading?
You can start with as little as ₹500–₹1,000, but more capital makes risk control easier. Margin and leverage amplify both gains and losses.
Do I need to pay taxes on intraday profits?
Intraday profits are often treated as speculative business income. Confirm with a professional for your facts, and keep records. See Tax overview.
Can I hold intraday positions overnight?
No. Intraday trades must be closed the same day. If not, your broker will square them off automatically.
How to pick stocks for intraday trading?
Look for stocks with high liquidity, volatility, and trading volume. Nifty 50 or large-cap stocks are common choices.
Where can I learn intraday trading strategies?
Use broker education, free courses, books, and practice on paper-trading accounts.
