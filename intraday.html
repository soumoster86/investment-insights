Intraday trading overview

Educational guide to same-day trading concepts, leverage risk, and terminology. Most beginners lose money with leverage — this page is not a trading system.

What is intraday trading?

Intraday trading means opening and closing positions in the same session (e.g. equity MIS products). The goal is short-term price moves, not multi-year ownership. Brokers square off open intraday positions before close.

Why people try it: Potential for quick gains, use of margin products, full use of the trading day.
Why it is hard: Costs + taxes + leverage can erase small edges; emotional decisions; forced square-off.

How it works

  1. Choose an intraday product (often labelled MIS / intraday) on your broker.
  2. Buy or sell with available margin — leverage amplifies both gains and losses.
  3. Close the position yourself or face broker square-off near market close.
  4. P&L settles for the day (plus charges); overnight investment risk differs from MIS.

Glossary

  • Margin: Borrowed funds from broker to trade larger positions than your balance allows.
  • Square Off: Closing an open position (buy/sell) within the same trading day.
  • Stop-Loss: An order set to automatically sell a position at a preset price to limit loss.
  • Volume: Number of shares traded during a given period. High volume = high liquidity.
  • Volatility: Degree of variation in stock price within a period—higher volatility means more price swings.
  • Leverage: Using borrowed money to increase potential return (and risk).
  • Order Book: Real-time list of buy and sell orders for a particular stock.
  • Bid-Ask Spread: Difference between the highest price a buyer will pay and the lowest price a seller will accept.
  • Intraday High/Low: Highest and lowest price of a stock during the trading day.
  • Scalping: Intraday strategy involving multiple trades for small gains.

Risk capital sketch

If you still experiment, cap the day’s risk. Educational only — not a strategy.

Strategy names (concepts only)

Labels traders use — none are guaranteed edges after costs.

  • Momentum: Follow strong short-term moves; exits when momentum fades.
  • Breakout: Enter on range breaks with volume (false breaks are common).
  • Mean reversion: Fade extremes — can fail in strong trends.
  • Scalping: Many tiny trades; fees and speed dominate.

Risk management tips

  • Only risk capital you can lose without harming goals or rent.
  • Define stop-loss before entry; avoid revenge trading.
  • Prefer liquid names; watch total costs (brokerage, STT, GST, etc.).
  • Journal trades; if net expectancy is negative, stop.
  • Long-term wealth usually comes from investing, not day trading — Stocks guide.

Common mistakes

  • No stop-loss; oversized leverage
  • Overtrading / chasing losses after a red day
  • Ignoring costs and taxes
  • Illiquid / penny stocks
  • Treating tips as research

FAQs

Is intraday suitable for beginners?
Usually a poor first step. Most people should master basics and long-term investing first. Paper trade if you must experiment.
What is the minimum amount needed for intraday trading?
You can start with as little as ₹500–₹1,000, but higher capital allows better diversification and risk management. Remember, margin/leverage can amplify both gains and losses.
Do I need to pay taxes on intraday profits?
Often treated as speculative business income under tax discussions — confirm with a professional for your facts. Keep records. See Tax overview.
Can I hold intraday positions overnight?
No. Intraday trades must be closed the same day. If not, your broker will square them off automatically.
How to pick stocks for intraday trading?
Look for stocks with high liquidity, volatility, and trading volume. Nifty 50 or large-cap stocks are common choices.
Where can I learn intraday trading strategies?
Use broker-provided education, free courses, YouTube, books, and practice on paper-trading accounts.
