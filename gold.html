Beginner path · step 9a

Gold investing in India

Gold is part of Indian culture and also used as a financial asset. This page compares jewellery, coins and bars, gold ETFs, and bonds, and how gold can sit next to stocks and fixed income. For learning only. Not a buy or sell call.

Why people allocate to gold

  • 🎯Diversification: Gold prices often move differently from stocks over some periods. That is not always true, and it is not guaranteed.
  • 💱Inflation and currency worry: Many people treat gold as a store of value when paper currencies weaken. Past performance is not a promise.
  • 🛟Everyday use: Weddings, gifts, and loans against physical metal.
  • 🏦Stress periods: Demand can rise in crises, but timing that well is very hard.
On this site: The home page shows a gold price snapshot (via a server proxy). Spot prices change all the time. Treat them as background context, not a trading signal.

Common ways to hold gold

Same metal idea, but costs, lock-ins, and who you rely on differ a lot.

💍

Jewellery

Making charges, design premiums, and buyback discounts often make jewellery a weak pure investment, even when the metal price rises. It still has personal and cultural value.

🪙

Coins and bars

Closer to bullion when purity and dealer spreads are clear. Storage, certificates, and resale spreads still matter. Prefer known purity standards and reputable dealers.

📊

Gold ETFs / gold mutual funds

Products that aim to track gold prices after costs. You need KYC, and ETFs usually need a Demat account. Expense ratios, tracking difference, and liquidity all affect what you earn. See the ETFs guide.

📜

Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB) and similar schemes

When open for issue, government-linked gold bonds have usually paid gold-price-linked redemption plus a small interest amount. Tenure and tax rules depend on the scheme. Issuance windows, interest rates, and tax rules are set by authorities. Always read the current term sheet. Our Bonds page covers SGB-style features in brief.

📱

“Digital gold” apps

Easy apps that claim gram-level ownership. Who holds the gold, how it is stored, and how the product is regulated all vary. Read the fine print carefully. These are not the same as ETFs or SGBs.

Role in a portfolio

Many allocation guides treat gold as a small side holding, not a substitute for emergency cash or a diversified equity core. See Risk & allocation for planning by time horizon.

  • 🎯Too little gold: you may miss diversification if that is what you want from it.
  • 📅Too much gold: over long periods you may lag equity growth needed for retirement-style goals. That is a historical pattern, not a guarantee.
Not a prediction: Gold can fall for long periods in rupees or after inflation. Do not borrow against it, and do not use money you will need soon for speculative gold bets.

For learning only. Past gold moves do not guarantee future prices.

Risks and costs to respect

  • ⚠️Price swings and long flat stretches
  • 🥇Currency effects (INR vs global dollar gold prices)
  • 💸Making charges, GST on some purchases, and bid–ask spreads
  • 💸ETF expense ratios and tracking difference
  • 📅Theft and storage risk for physical metal
  • 🧾Tax and TDS rules that depend on the product

Common mistakes

  • 💎Buying high-making-charge jewellery and treating the full bill as “investment returns”
  • 📈Going all-in on gold after a price spike because of FOMO
  • 🛟Ignoring insurance and emergency funds while piling into metal
  • 🚫Treating gold as a guaranteed real return
