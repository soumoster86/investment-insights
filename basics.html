Beginner path · step 1

How to start investing in India

A plain path from zero to your first SIP: accounts, KYC, and simple first steps. This is educational only, not personalised financial advice.

Why invest at all?

Money that only sits in a savings account often grows slower than prices rise. That gap is inflation. Investing means putting money into things like mutual funds, stocks, deposits, or bonds so it can work toward goals: an emergency buffer, a home, education, or retirement.

Mindset: Start small. Stay consistent. Think in years while you learn, not days.

Do this before you invest

  1. 🛟Build a basic emergency fund. Keep a few months of essentials somewhere easy to access, so a crisis does not force you to sell investments.
  2. 💳Clear high-interest consumer debt (for example credit-card dues) when the interest you pay is likely higher than what investing might earn.
  3. 🛡️Protect dependants with term life if they rely on your income. Insurance is protection, not a stand-in for investing.
  4. 🎯Write down goals (amount and year). Use the Goal planner to sketch a SIP.

Accounts you typically need

Bank account

A regular savings account with UPI or net banking is the base for payments and SIP auto-debits.

KYC (Know Your Customer)

KYC checks who you are (usually PAN, Aadhaar, photo, and signature or eSign). Complete it once with a mutual fund platform, broker, or KRA, then you can invest across products.

Demat & trading account (for stocks, ETFs, many bonds)

  • 🔐Demat holds securities electronically, like a locker for shares.
  • 📊Trading is the account you use to place buy and sell orders on the exchange.
  • 🏦You usually open both together with a SEBI-registered broker (examples people use: Zerodha, Groww, Upstox, bank brokers, and others).

Mutual fund folio (can be without Demat)

You can start many mutual fund SIPs with only KYC and a bank account through a fund house app, MF utility, or investment platform. Demat is optional for mutual funds.

Safety: Stick to regulated platforms. Never share OTPs or passwords. Unsolicited “guaranteed return” tips are a red flag.

A sensible first investment: SIP in a diversified fund

For many beginners, a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) in a diversified equity or hybrid mutual fund is simpler than picking stocks one by one. Once you have a Demat, a broad market index fund or ETF can also fit that role.

  1. 🪪Finish KYC on a trusted platform.
  2. 🧭Choose a fund category you understand (large-cap, flexi-cap, or index). Check expense ratio and risk before you pick.
  3. 💸Start a SIP with an amount you can keep paying even in a tight month.
  4. 📅Automate the debit. Review once or twice a year, not every day.

Try numbers first with the SIP calculator and Target SIP tool.

Beginner checklist

  • 🪪PAN and bank account ready
  • ✅KYC completed
  • 🛟Emergency money set aside
  • 🎯Goal written down, even roughly
  • 🌱First SIP set up (small is fine)
  • 📂You know how to log in and find statements or folios

Common early mistakes

  • ⏱️Putting money you need in the next few months into equities
  • 🔥Chasing last year’s top fund or hot tips
  • 📱Checking the portfolio daily and panic-stopping SIPs
  • 📚Buying many overlapping funds (a collection, not a plan)
  • 🔀Mixing insurance and investment products without checking the costs
