Real estate investing basics

How residential property, land, and listed REITs fit (or don’t fit) next to equity SIPs and fixed income. Educational only — not a buy/sell call on any project, city, or developer.

Why people allocate to real estate

  • 📈Use + asset: Primary home solves housing needs while building equity over a long loan.
  • 📅Leverage narrative: Home loans let buyers control a larger asset with a down payment — leverage amplifies gains and stress.
  • 💼Rental income story: Yields vary widely by city, micro-location, and vacancy; gross yield is not take-home cash.
  • 🏠Inflation / “hard asset” narrative: Property prices can rise over long periods — they can also stagnate or fall in real terms.
Portfolio framing: For many households, a self-occupied home is a lifestyle goal first. Treat extra investment property as a concentrated, illiquid bet — see Risk & allocation.

True costs beyond the sticker price

Agreement value is only the start. Buyers often underestimate stamp duty, registration, GST (where applicable), brokerage, interiors, society deposits, and ongoing maintenance.

  • 🛟Stamp duty & registration — state-specific; can be a large cash outlay on day one.
  • 🧾GST / taxes — depend on project type and current law (especially under-construction).
  • 💸Loan costs — processing fees, insurance add-ons, floating-rate resets.
  • 🧾Holding costs — property tax, society charges, repairs, vacancy between tenants.
  • 📅Exit costs — brokerage, capital-gains tax treatment, time on market.
Yield reality check: Rental yield is often quoted as annual rent ÷ price. After tax, maintenance, and vacancy, net yield can be far lower — and still requires your time.

Home loans & EMI (high level)

  • 🏠Most buyers use a floating-rate home loan linked to a benchmark; EMIs can change when rates move.
  • 📅EMI is the monthly instalment; early years pay more interest than principal on long tenures.
  • 💼Eligibility depends on income, credit score, existing EMIs, and lender policy — not just the property price.
  • 🏠Try rough numbers with our EMI calculator before talking to a bank.
Stress test: Ask whether you can still pay if rates rise 1–2% or income is interrupted for a few months. Keep an emergency fund separate from the down payment — Emergency & insurance.

REITs (listed real estate)

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) in India are SEBI-regulated vehicles that typically own income-producing commercial real estate and distribute a large share of cash flows. You buy units on the exchange (subject to liquidity), not a specific flat.

  • ⚠️Different risk profile from residential land or under-construction flats
  • 📈Prices move with rates, occupancy, and market mood — units can trade at premium/discount to NAV narratives
  • 📅Tax and distribution rules are product- and year-specific — verify official documents

For fund-like diversification without property paperwork, some investors compare REITs with mutual funds / ETFs rather than with a second home.

Risks to respect

  • 🛟Illiquidity — selling can take months; price is negotiated, not a live NAV.
  • 📈Concentration — one property can be a huge share of net worth in one city/micro-market.
  • 💼Leverage risk — job loss + high EMI is a common household stress path.
  • ⚠️Title / legal risk — incomplete paperwork, disputes, or non-compliant projects.
  • 🏠Construction delay — especially under-construction primary sales.
  • 🛟Opportunity cost — cash locked in property is cash not in diversified equity/debt SIPs.
  • 🧾Tax complexity — capital gains, rent, interest deductions: see Tax overview (not advice).

Common mistakes

  • 📈Buying purely for FOMO after a local price spike without a 10+ year plan
  • 😰Stretching EMI beyond a comfortable share of take-home pay
  • 🧾Ignoring stamp duty, interiors, and vacancy when “calculating yield”
  • 📋Skipping RERA / title / society due diligence for a “good deal”
  • 🏗️Treating under-construction inventory as liquid savings
  • 💳Owning multiple leveraged properties with no emergency cash
