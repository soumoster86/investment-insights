Beginner path · step 9b

Real estate investing basics

How residential property, land, and listed REITs compare with equity SIPs and fixed income. For learning only. Not a buy or sell call on any project, city, or developer.

Why people allocate to real estate

  • 📈Home and asset: A primary home meets housing needs while you build equity over a long loan.
  • 📅Leverage: A home loan lets you control a larger asset with a down payment. Leverage magnifies gains and stress.
  • 💼Rental income: Yields vary a lot by city, location, and vacancy. Gross yield is not take-home cash.
  • 🏠Hard asset: Property prices can rise over long periods. They can also stagnate or fall after inflation.
How to think about it: For many households, a self-occupied home is a lifestyle goal first. Treat extra investment property as a concentrated, hard-to-sell bet. See Risk & allocation.

True costs beyond the sticker price

Agreement value is only the start. Buyers often underestimate stamp duty, registration, GST where it applies, brokerage, interiors, society deposits, and ongoing maintenance.

  • 🛟Stamp duty and registration: Set by the state, and can be a large cash outlay on day one.
  • 🧾GST and taxes: Depend on the project type and current law, especially for under-construction homes.
  • 💸Loan costs: Processing fees, insurance add-ons, and floating-rate resets.
  • 🧾Holding costs: Property tax, society charges, repairs, and vacancy between tenants.
  • 📅Exit costs: Brokerage, capital-gains tax, and time on the market.
Yield reality check: Rental yield is often quoted as annual rent divided by price. After tax, maintenance, and vacancy, net yield can be much lower. It still takes your time.

Home loans & EMI (high level)

  • 🏠Most buyers use a floating-rate home loan linked to a benchmark. EMIs can change when rates move.
  • 📅EMI is the monthly instalment. On long tenures, early years pay more interest than principal.
  • 💼Eligibility depends on income, credit score, existing EMIs, and lender policy, not just the property price.
  • 🏠Try rough numbers with the home buy cost calculator below, or the standalone EMI tool.
Stress test: Ask whether you can still pay if rates rise 1–2% or income is interrupted for a few months. Keep an emergency fund separate from the down payment. See Emergency & insurance.

REITs (listed real estate)

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) in India are SEBI-regulated vehicles that usually own income-producing commercial property and pay out a large share of cash flows. You buy units on the exchange (liquidity varies), not a specific flat.

  • ⚠️Risk profile differs from residential land or under-construction flats
  • 📈Prices move with rates, occupancy, and market mood. Units can trade above or below reported NAV
  • 📅Tax and distribution rules depend on the product and year. Check official documents

If you want fund-like diversification without property paperwork, some people compare REITs with mutual funds or ETFs rather than with a second home.

Risks to respect

  • 🛟Illiquidity: Selling can take months. Price is negotiated, not a live NAV.
  • 📈Concentration: One property can be a huge share of net worth in one city or locality.
  • 💼Leverage risk: Job loss plus a high EMI is a common household stress path.
  • ⚠️Title and legal risk: Incomplete paperwork, disputes, or non-compliant projects.
  • 🏠Construction delay: Common on under-construction primary sales.
  • 🛟Opportunity cost: Cash locked in property is cash not in diversified equity or debt SIPs.
  • 🧾Tax complexity: Capital gains, rent, and interest deductions. See Tax overview (not advice).

Common mistakes

  • 📈Buying on FOMO after a local price spike with no 10+ year plan
  • 😰Stretching EMI beyond a comfortable share of take-home pay
  • 🧾Ignoring stamp duty, interiors, and vacancy when calculating yield
  • 📋Skipping RERA, title, and society checks for a “good deal”
  • 🏗️Treating under-construction inventory as liquid savings
  • 💳Owning multiple leveraged properties with no emergency cash
