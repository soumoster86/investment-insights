What is NPS?

National Pension Scheme (NPS) is a government-backed retirement savings scheme in India that helps individuals build a retirement corpus through disciplined long-term investing.

It is regulated by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) and offers a mix of equity, corporate bonds, and government securities.

Key Features

  • Low-cost retirement investment
  • Market-linked returns (Equity + Debt)
  • Flexible asset allocation
  • Long-term wealth creation
  • Available to all Indian citizens (18–70 years)

Tax Benefits

  • ₹1.5L deduction under Section 80C
  • Additional ₹50,000 under Section 80CCD(1B)
  • Employer contribution benefit under 80CCD(2)

Types of NPS Accounts

  • Tier I: Mandatory retirement account (lock-in till 60)
  • Tier II: Optional, flexible withdrawal account

Benefits of Investing in NPS

  • Disciplined retirement savings
  • Very low fund management cost
  • Tax-efficient investment
  • Diversified portfolio
  • Long-term compounding benefits

Limitations

  • Lock-in till retirement age (60 years)
  • Partial withdrawal restrictions
  • Mandatory annuity purchase at maturity
  • Returns are market-dependent

How to Invest in NPS

  • Register online via NSDL or Karvy portal
  • Open NPS account through banks or brokers
  • Select fund manager and asset allocation
  • Start contributing regularly

Who Should Invest?

  • Salaried individuals looking for tax savings
  • Long-term retirement planners
  • Investors seeking low-cost diversified exposure

💡 Pro Tip

Use NPS mainly for tax saving + retirement discipline, not as your only investment. Combine it with mutual funds for better flexibility.
