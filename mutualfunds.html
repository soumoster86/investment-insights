Mutual funds guide & calculators

Learn how mutual funds work in India, how SIPs and SWPs differ, and try the calculators with your own numbers. Sample scheme names are for study only. This is not a recommendation to buy any fund.

What is a mutual fund?

A mutual fund pools money from many investors and invests in equities, debt, gold, or other assets under a stated mandate. You own units priced each business day at a NAV (net asset value). Compared with buying many stocks yourself, a fund gives you ready diversification and either professional stock-picking or a rules-based index. You still pay fees, and market risk still applies.

Why people use them: Diversification, SIPs that build a habit, low minimums, and easy access through apps, AMCs, or MF Utility.
What can go wrong: NAV can fall. Active managers can lag their benchmark. Expense ratios chip away at returns over time. Past returns do not guarantee the future.

SIP vs lumpsum (concept)

📅

SIP

Cash flow
Fixed amount on a regular schedule
Behaviour
Builds a habit and averages your purchase NAV over time
Who often uses
People who save from a salary each month

Fits well when income arrives monthly

💰

Lumpsum

Cash flow
One-time investment
Behaviour
Results depend on when you enter and whether you stay invested
Who often uses
Bonus, inheritance, or cash after a rebalance

Useful when a large sum is already available

Neither is always better. Match the method to when the money is available and how long you can invest. Try numbers with the SIP calculator below, the lumpsum tool, or the goal planner.

How to invest (practical path)

  1. 🪪Complete KYC with PAN, Aadhaar, and bank details on a regulated platform or AMC app.
  2. 🎯Choose a category that matches your goal and risk, not last year’s top return alone.
  3. 🔍Compare expense ratio, AUM, tracking (for index funds), and consistency. Don’t rely on star ratings alone.
  4. 💸Start a SIP or lumpsum you can stick with, and keep records of your investments.
  5. 📅Review once or twice a year. Avoid chasing every new fund launch.
  • 🛟Build an emergency fund first. See Emergency & insurance.
  • 🎯Set your asset mix by time horizon. See Risk & allocation.
  • 🧾Tax basics: Tax overview and PPF & tax-saving.

Key terms

  • ✨NAV: price per unit, usually updated each business day.
  • 💸Expense ratio: ongoing fee taken from the fund (and thus from your returns).
  • 💸Exit load: fee if you redeem within a stated period.
  • 💸Direct vs regular: the commission channel changes what you pay.
  • 📅AUM: assets under management (size of the fund).
  • ⭐Benchmark: the index used to judge performance.
  • 🏦SWP: systematic withdrawal of a fixed amount.
  • 🧭STP: systematic transfer between schemes (rules vary by product).

Common mistakes

  • 📚Owning many equity funds that mostly hold the same stocks (higher fees, little extra diversification)
  • 🏆Chasing last year’s top performer and switching every year (taxes and bad habits add up)
  • 💸Ignoring expense ratio and the cost of regular vs direct plans
  • 🛑Stopping SIPs only because markets fell, when your goal is still years away
  • ⏱️Putting money you need in a few months into equity funds

Practical tips

  • 🌱Start early and stay consistent. SIPs work best with time, not constant tinkering.
  • 🎯Match equity share to how long you can stay invested. See Risk & allocation.
  • 🏠Build a simple core first (a broad index or diversified fund) before niche sector funds.
  • 🔄Review once or twice a year. Rebalance if your plan weights drift.
  • 🧮Put numbers next to your goals with the goal planner.
