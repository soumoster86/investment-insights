Global equities · for Indian residents

US stocks from India

Diversify beyond domestic markets via US equities and ETFs — understand LRS, currency risk, tax notes, and access routes. Educational only; not a recommendation to buy any foreign security.

About the US market

The NYSE and NASDAQ list many of the world’s largest companies. Benchmarks Indians often hear about include the S&P 500, Nasdaq-100, and Dow Jones.

  • 🧭S&P 500: Broad large-cap US equities
  • 📈Nasdaq-100: Tech-heavy large growth names
  • 🔎Dow Jones: 30 blue-chip industrial / consumer names

Why Indian investors look at the US

Possible reasons: Global diversification, sector leaders (tech, healthcare), dollar exposure.
Trade-offs: Currency risk (USD/INR), LRS limits & TCS discussions, platform fees, tax complexity, and still full equity market risk.

How Indians typically access US stocks

  1. Direct stocks/ETFs via brokers offering international investing (examples people search: INDmoney, Vested, some full-service brokers) — subject to KYC and LRS.
  2. India-listed international ETFs / FoFs that hold overseas indices (product availability varies).
  3. Mutual funds with global mandates (read scheme documents).

Platform names are examples, not endorsements. Compare fees, forex spreads, and support.

LRS, costs & compliance (high level)

  • 📅Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) caps resident individuals’ overseas remittances per financial year (limit set by RBI — verify current figure).
  • 🧾TCS / bank processes may apply above thresholds under current tax rules — confirm with your bank/CA.
  • 💸Costs: FX conversion, platform commissions, custody, and bid–ask on US ETFs add up.
  • 🧾Keep records of all remittances and trades for tax filing.

See also Tax overview.

Risks & tax notes (high level)

Not tax or forex advice. Capital gains, dividends, FTC, TCS, and LRS limits change. Confirm with RBI/bank, Income Tax materials, and a qualified professional.
  • ⚠️Currency risk: USD/INR moves can dominate rupee returns.
  • 📅Equity risk: US markets can fall hard for years.
  • 🧾Tax: Indian tax on foreign stocks/ETFs depends on holding period and current statute; US withholding may apply on dividends (W-8BEN discussions).
  • 📅LRS / TCS: Remittance caps and tax collection rules apply — verify current year figures before remitting.
  • ⚠️Platform risk: Custody, fees, and support quality vary.

Practical tips

  • 📦Prefer diversified US ETFs before many single stocks if research time is limited.
  • 💱Include FX and all-in fees in your expected return math.
  • 🎯Size the overseas sleeve as part of overall asset allocation — Risk & allocation.
  • 📁Keep records of every remittance and sale for filing season.

Common mistakes

  • 💱Ignoring currency drag/boost when comparing to Nifty returns
  • 🖥️Concentrating only in a few mega-cap tech names
  • 🧾Underestimating total costs and tax paperwork
  • ✈️Remitting more than planned without an LRS/TCS checklist
