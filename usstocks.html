Global equities · for Indian residents

US stocks from India

How Indian residents can look at US equities and ETFs: LRS, currency risk, tax notes, and common access routes. For learning only. Not a recommendation to buy any foreign security.

About the US market

The NYSE and NASDAQ list many of the world’s largest companies. Benchmarks Indians often hear about include the S&P 500, Nasdaq-100, and Dow Jones.

  • 🧭S&P 500: A broad set of large US companies
  • 📈Nasdaq-100: Large growth names with a heavy tech weight
  • 🔎Dow Jones: 30 well-known industrial and consumer companies

Why Indian investors look at the US

Possible reasons: Global diversification, exposure to sector leaders (tech, healthcare), and some dollar exposure.
Trade-offs: Currency risk (USD/INR), LRS limits and TCS, platform fees, more tax paperwork, and full equity market risk.

How Indians typically access US stocks

  1. 🌐Direct stocks/ETFs through brokers that offer international investing (for example INDmoney, Vested, or some full-service brokers), subject to KYC and LRS.
  2. 📊India-listed international ETFs / FoFs that hold overseas indices. Availability varies.
  3. 🧺Mutual funds with global mandates. Read the scheme documents.

Platform names are examples, not endorsements. Compare fees, forex spreads, and support quality.

LRS, costs & compliance (high level)

  • 📅Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) caps how much resident individuals can send overseas each financial year. The limit is set by RBI. Check the current figure.
  • 🧾TCS and bank processes may apply above thresholds under current tax rules. Confirm with your bank or CA.
  • 💸Costs: FX conversion, platform commissions, custody, and bid–ask spreads on US ETFs add up.
  • 🧾Keep records of all remittances and trades for tax filing.

See also Tax overview.

Risks & tax notes (high level)

Not tax or forex advice. Capital gains, dividends, FTC, TCS, and LRS limits change. Confirm with RBI or your bank, Income Tax materials, and a qualified professional.
  • ⚠️Currency risk: USD/INR moves can dominate rupee returns.
  • 📅Equity risk: US markets can fall hard for years.
  • 🧾Tax: Indian tax on foreign stocks/ETFs depends on holding period and current law. US withholding may apply on dividends (often handled via W-8BEN).
  • 📅LRS / TCS: Remittance caps and tax collection rules apply. Check current-year figures before you remit.
  • ⚠️Platform risk: Custody, fees, and support quality vary.

Practical tips

  • 📦If research time is limited, prefer diversified US ETFs before many single stocks.
  • 💱Include FX and all-in fees when you estimate returns.
  • 🎯Size the overseas holding as part of your full allocation. See Risk & allocation.
  • 📁Keep records of every remittance and sale for filing season.

Common mistakes

  • 💱Ignoring currency moves when comparing to Nifty returns
  • 🖥️Putting everything into a few mega-cap tech names
  • 🧾Underestimating total costs and tax paperwork
  • ✈️Remitting more than planned without checking LRS and TCS first
