About US Market

The United States is home to the world’s largest and most influential stock markets—the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and NASDAQ. The US stock market is the backbone of global investing, with indices like the S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, and Dow Jones Industrial Average setting the tone for international investors.

  • S&P 500: 500 largest US companies, represents over 80% of total US market cap.
  • NASDAQ 100: Tech-heavy, includes Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Nvidia, Tesla, and more.
  • Dow Jones: 30 blue-chip US companies, oldest stock index in the US.

With a market cap exceeding $45 trillion, the US market offers innovation, global leadership, and sectoral depth unmatched by any other economy.

Why Invest in US Stocks?

  • Access to world’s largest and most innovative companies
  • Diversification outside India for currency & geo-risk
  • Participate in global trends (AI, Tech, EVs, Healthcare)
  • Potential for strong long-term dollar returns

How to Invest in US Stocks from India?

  • Use Indian brokers with global access: IndMoney, Motilal Oswal, HDFC Securities, etc.
  • Invest in US-focused mutual funds or ETFs (like Nasdaq 100 ETF)
  • Open accounts with global platforms (e.g., Vested, INDmoney)
  • Be aware of LRS ($250,000/year limit) and RBI rules

Taxation & Risks for Indian Investors

  • Currency Risk: US investments are in USD; INR-USD exchange rate fluctuations can impact your returns (positively or negatively).
  • Tax on US Stocks: Capital gains from US stocks/ETFs are taxed in India as per your income slab (if held <2 years), and at 20% with indexation for >2 years. No tax in US on capital gains for Indians.
  • Dividend Tax: US deducts 25% withholding tax on dividends (post W-8BEN form). Dividends are taxed again in India (with foreign tax credit).
  • LRS Limit: You can remit up to $250,000 per financial year (under RBI’s Liberalized Remittance Scheme).
  • Remittance Tax (TCS): Tax Collected at Source (TCS) is levied on overseas investments above ₹7 lakh/year. You can claim this back while filing ITR.
  • Other Risks: US market volatility, regulatory risk, geo-political events, and unfamiliarity with US companies/sectors.
Tip: Consult a tax advisor for the latest tax rules before investing in US markets.

Tips for US Equity Investing

  • Start with US ETFs or Index Funds for easy diversification
  • Track currency conversion (INR-USD) costs & taxes
  • Monitor US earnings, Fed news, global macro trends
  • Beware of time-zone differences for trading
