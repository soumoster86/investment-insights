Stocks guide & tools

Learn how shares work, how to start on NSE/BSE, try a simple trade P&L calculator, and keep a watchlist in your browser. This is not personalised advice.

What is a stock?

A stock (share) is a unit of ownership in a company. When you buy shares listed on exchanges like the NSE or BSE, you can gain if the business grows (the share price rises) and, for some companies, from dividends. Prices move every trading day, sometimes a lot. Equities suit money you can leave invested for years, not emergency cash.

Why people invest: Room for long-term growth, easy to buy and sell on the exchange, and a way to own a stake in Indian (and global) businesses.
Risks: Markets and companies can go against you. Returns are not guaranteed. You can lose money permanently if a business fails.

Orders & metrics beginners hear about

Order types (simplified)

  • 📈Market order: buy or sell at the best available price now. Speed over price control.
  • 💧Limit order: you set a max buy or min sell price. The order may not fill.
  • ⚠️Stop-loss (concept): meant to limit losses if price moves against you. Exact product names vary by broker.

Ratios (glossary, not magic numbers)

  • 📈Market cap: share price × shares outstanding (a size proxy).
  • 📈P/E: price relative to earnings. High or low alone means little without growth and peer context.
  • 📅EPS: earnings per share. It can be noisy year to year.
  • ⭐Dividend yield: dividend ÷ price. Yields can fall if the price rises or dividends are cut.
Practice: Read a company’s annual report or exchange filings and compare with peers before you act. Screeners and social tips are starting points, not conclusions.

How to invest in stocks in India

  1. 🪪Have your PAN and bank account ready for KYC.
  2. 🏦Open a Demat and trading account with a SEBI-registered broker.
  3. ✍️Complete KYC (eSign or video KYC, as offered).
  4. 💸Fund the account and place a small practice order you understand.
  5. 📝Write a simple plan: goal, time horizon, max loss you can tolerate, and diversification rules.

Starter checklist

  • 🛟Handle your emergency fund and high-interest debt first. See Emergency & insurance.
  • ⚡Know the difference between investing and intraday speculation. See the intraday overview.
  • 🧺Consider broad index ETFs or funds before a long list of single stocks. See ETFs.
  • 🧾Skim tax basics at a high level. See the tax overview.

Common mistakes

  • 💬Buying on tips without understanding the business or how long you can hold
  • 🎯Putting most of your savings in one stock or one sector
  • ⚡Using leverage or F&O without experience (you can lose money fast)
  • 📱Checking prices every hour and panic-selling long-term holdings
  • 🛟Ignoring costs, taxes, and the need for an emergency fund

Practical tips

  • 🎯Match equity exposure to how many years until you need the money. See Risk & allocation.
  • 🧺Keep a simple diversified core. Use single stocks only for amounts you can research.
  • 📆Review yearly, not daily. Rebalance if weights drift far from your plan.
  • ✍️Write rules before markets get stressful: what you’ll buy, hold, or sell.
