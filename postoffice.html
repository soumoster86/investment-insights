Cash & deposits · India

Post Office savings schemes

A plain map of common India Post small-savings products, how they differ from bank FDs, and a simple calculator to try numbers. Rates are notified by the government and change over time. This page is for learning only, not a product offer from India Post.

What are Post Office savings schemes?

The Department of Posts (with the Government of India small-savings framework) offers savings accounts and fixed-horizon deposits that many households use for safety and regular income. You open most of them at a post office (or, for some products, also at authorised banks).

Why people use them: Sovereign-linked products, wide branch reach, clear tenures, and options for monthly interest or forced monthly saving (RD).
Limits: Returns may lag long-run equity after inflation and tax. Some products have lock-ins or caps. Interest rates are reset for new deposits when the government revises them.

Related reading: Bank FDs, PPF & tax-saving (PPF can also be held at post offices), and Emergency fund for short-term cash needs.

Who often considers these schemes?

  • 🛟People who want a large share of money in government-linked small savings
  • 📅Monthly savers who like RD discipline more than a lump-sum FD
  • 💸Retirees exploring MIS or SCSS for regular interest (eligibility rules apply)
  • 🏦Households in areas where the post office is easier to reach than a bank branch
Portfolio framing: Treat PO deposits as a conservative sleeve next to equity SIPs and an emergency fund — not as a substitute for long-term growth assets if you need inflation-beating returns over decades. See Risk & allocation.

Tax notes (high level)

Not tax advice. Interest on many PO products is taxable under current law, though some products or contributions have special treatments in some years. TDS rules can apply. Confirm for your financial year.
  • 🧾Interest often counts as income — keep passbook and 26AS/AIS records.
  • 📘NSC interest is sometimes discussed in tax-saving conversations; treatment is statute-specific.
  • 🔗Broader map: Tax overview · PPF & tax-saving.

Common mistakes

  • 📰Using last year’s rate for a new deposit without checking the current circular
  • 🔓Parking emergency money in a long lock-in product
  • 📈Expecting FD-like products to beat long-run equity after inflation
  • 🧾Ignoring tax on interest when comparing “higher” PO rates to other options
  • 🧓Opening SCSS without checking age and deposit-cap rules
