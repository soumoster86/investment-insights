Digital assets · high risk

Cryptocurrencies guide & tools

Learn what crypto is, why it is extremely volatile, how India currently approaches tax and compliance at a high level, and use sizing / P&L sketches — not a recommendation to buy any coin.

What are cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrencies are digital assets that typically use cryptography and shared ledgers (blockchains) to record transfers without a traditional bank as the middleman. Some networks also host applications (smart contracts, DeFi, NFTs). As an investment class they behave more like speculative risk assets than bank deposits or diversified equity funds.

Why people explore them: 24/7 markets, global transfer narratives, programmable finance experiments, and historical high upside for early holders of large networks.
Why many avoid large allocations: Extreme drawdowns, exchange/wallet hacks, scams, operational complexity, and evolving regulation / tax rules.

Common categories (map)

Category Idea Risk note
Large payment / store-of-value coins e.g. Bitcoin-style networks often called “digital gold” in media Still highly volatile vs gold or FDs
Smart-contract platforms Host apps, tokens, DeFi Tech + market + smart-contract risk
Exchange / utility tokens Linked to exchange ecosystems Business + regulatory concentration
Stablecoins Peg narratives to fiat Peg can break; issuer / reserve risk
Meme / micro caps Narrative-driven tokens Often near-zero research quality; can go to zero

Risks to take seriously

  • Price risk — 50–80% drawdowns have been common historically for many assets.
  • Custody risk — lost keys, phishing, exchange insolvency can mean permanent loss.
  • Operational risk — wrong network, wrong address, irreversible sends.
  • Liquidity risk — thin markets for small tokens; large spreads.
  • Regulatory / tax risk — rules evolve; compliance failures are costly.
  • Scam risk — fake apps, giveaways, rug pulls, social engineering.
Position sizing rule of thumb (educational): only capital you can afford to lose entirely. Complete core goals (emergency fund, boring equity/debt SIPs) before speculative sleeves — Emergency fund · Risk & allocation.

India context (high level)

Not legal or tax advice. Virtual digital assets (VDAs) have been subject to specific tax and TDS-style provisions. Thresholds and rates change — verify with the Income Tax Department or a qualified professional.
  • Gains on VDAs have been taxed under special rules in recent years (distinct from classic equity LTCG memory).
  • Transfers may involve TDS / reporting — ignore social-media “no tax” myths.
  • Exchanges may register / comply with evolving FIU and other requirements — prefer transparent, compliant platforms.
  • Banking rails and product availability can change with regulation.

Security hygiene

  • Enable 2FA; never share OTPs or seed phrases
  • Bookmark official URLs; avoid “support” DMs
  • Consider self-custody only if you understand recovery risk
  • Start with tiny amounts until processes feel safe

Common mistakes

  • Borrowing to buy crypto or using leverage without experience
  • All-in after a big price run (FOMO)
  • Keeping large balances on exchanges long-term without a custody plan
  • Clicking airdrop / recovery links from strangers
  • Ignoring Indian tax/TDS obligations on virtual digital assets
  • Skipping emergency funds and diversified SIPs for speculative tokens
