Digital assets · high risk

Cryptocurrencies guide & tools

What crypto is, why prices swing so hard, how India currently treats tax and compliance at a high level, and simple sizing / P&L tools. This is not a recommendation to buy any coin.

What are cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrencies are digital assets that usually use cryptography and shared ledgers (blockchains) to record transfers without a traditional bank in the middle. Some networks also run apps such as smart contracts, DeFi, and NFTs. As investments they behave more like speculative risk assets than bank deposits or diversified equity funds.

Why people look at them: Markets run 24/7, cross-border transfers are easier in theory, some networks experiment with programmable finance, and early holders of large networks have seen large gains in the past.
Why many keep allocations small: Extreme price falls, exchange and wallet hacks, scams, operational complexity, and changing regulation and tax rules.

Risks to take seriously

  • 📉Price risk: Falls of 50–80% have been common for many assets in the past.
  • 🔐Custody risk: Lost keys, phishing, or exchange failure can mean permanent loss.
  • ⚙️Operational risk: Wrong network, wrong address, and irreversible transfers.
  • 💧Liquidity risk: Thin markets for small tokens and wide spreads.
  • 🧾Regulatory and tax risk: Rules change, and compliance mistakes are costly.
  • 🎣Scam risk: Fake apps, giveaways, rug pulls, and social engineering.
Simple sizing idea: Use only money you can afford to lose completely. Finish core goals first (emergency fund, steady equity and debt SIPs) before any speculative holding. See Emergency fund and Risk & allocation.

India context (high level)

Not legal or tax advice. Virtual digital assets (VDAs) have faced specific tax and TDS-style rules. Thresholds and rates change. Confirm with the Income Tax Department or a qualified professional.
  • 📅Gains on VDAs have been taxed under special rules in recent years. Those rules differ from classic equity LTCG.
  • 🧾Transfers may involve TDS or reporting. Ignore social-media “no tax” claims.
  • 💡Exchanges may need to meet FIU and other requirements. Prefer transparent, compliant platforms.
  • 🔎Banking access and product availability can change with regulation.

Basic security habits

  • 🔐Turn on 2FA. Never share OTPs or seed phrases.
  • 🔗Bookmark official URLs. Avoid “support” messages in DMs.
  • 🗝️Use self-custody only if you understand recovery risk.
  • 🧪Start with tiny amounts until the process feels safe.

Common mistakes

  • 💳Borrowing to buy crypto or using leverage without experience
  • 🎢Going all-in after a big price run (FOMO)
  • 🔐Leaving large balances on exchanges long-term with no custody plan
  • 🎣Clicking airdrop or recovery links from strangers
  • 🧾Ignoring Indian tax and TDS rules on virtual digital assets
  • 🛟Skipping emergency funds and diversified SIPs for speculative tokens
