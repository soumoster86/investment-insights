Protect & plan · Priority 4

Emergency fund & insurance basics

Before chasing higher returns, most households need a cash buffer and the right protection. This guide explains the ideas, not product picks or personalised advice.

Why “protect first” matters

Investing works best when you can stay invested through job loss, medical bills, or family emergencies. Without a buffer, people often redeem mutual funds or sell stocks at the worst time. Life and health insurance shift large, rare risks onto an insurer. They are not a substitute for SIPs, and they are not a way to “beat the market.”

Typical order: clear high-interest debt, build an emergency fund, buy essential protection if dependants or medical risk apply, then invest for goals. See also How to start.

Emergency fund

What it is

Money set aside for unexpected essential expenses. Not holidays, gadgets, or market “dips.” It should be easy to reach without brokerage delays or big price risk.

How much people often target

A common teaching range is 3–12 months of essential expenses (rent or EMI, food, utilities, school fees, insurance premiums, minimum debt payments). Single-income households, freelancers, and people with dependents often aim higher. Dual stable incomes with low fixed costs may start lower and grow into it.

Where to keep it

  • 🛟Savings account: easiest access; returns are usually low.
  • 🛟Sweep or liquid-style options: a bit more yield with quick access (product rules vary).
  • 🛟Short FDs or liquid/overnight funds: some people use these for the outer layers of the buffer. Access and low volatility still come first.
Usually not ideal for the core buffer: equity funds, individual stocks, crypto, or long-lock products (for example classic PPF) where you cannot get money quickly without cost or penalty.

How to build it

  1. 📝List monthly essentials honestly. Use real bills, not aspirational lifestyle spend.
  2. 🏦Park the buffer in a dedicated account so you do not “accidentally” spend it.
  3. 🔁Automate a transfer on salary day until you hit the target.
  4. ♻️Refill after you use it before you raise riskier investments.

For rough sizing, use the Goal planner or Target SIP with a short horizon and conservative assumptions. Treat the results as sketches only.

Term life insurance (concept)

Term cover pays a lump sum to nominees if the insured person dies during the policy term. Premiums are usually lower than for policies that mix insurance with investment or savings, because you are mainly buying pure risk cover.

  • 💼Who often needs it: people with dependents who rely on their income, or loans that family would struggle to service.
  • 👥Who may need less (or none): people with no financial dependents and enough assets already. Still a personal call, not a rule.
  • 💼Cover amount (idea): often framed as a multiple of annual expenses or income, plus major liabilities, minus liquid assets you already have. Work this through carefully, or with a licensed adviser.
Tip: Compare claim settlement ratios, exclusions, and insurer stability from public sources. Prefer clear term plans over “returns plus cover” bundles unless you fully understand the costs.

Health insurance (concept)

Medical costs can wipe out years of SIPs. A health policy is meant to pay or reimburse eligible hospitalisation and related expenses under the policy terms. It is not meant to grow wealth.

  • 🌍Check sum insured, room-rent limits, co-pay, waiting periods, and network hospitals.
  • 💼Employer cover helps, but it may end if you change jobs. Many people keep a personal policy as a base.
  • 🏠Family floater vs individual policies trade premium cost against how the sum insured is shared.

Rules, IRDAI product norms, and tax treatment of premiums can change. Read the policy wording and official insurer materials. This site does not sell or rate specific policies.

Common mistakes

  • 🛟Skipping the emergency fund to push equity SIPs early on
  • 🏥Under-insuring health while buying market-linked “ULIP-style” products without comparing costs
  • 💼Assuming employer cover is permanent
  • 📝Leaving no nominee, or never telling family where policies and accounts live
  • 🗣️Treating commission-driven sales pitches as independent advice
