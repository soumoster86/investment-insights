Fixed deposits guide & calculator

Learn how bank FDs work, what DICGC covers, estimate maturity with compounding choices, and sketch a simple FD ladder — educational only, not a rate offer from any bank.

What is a fixed deposit?

A fixed deposit (FD) is a deposit with a bank or NBFC for a chosen tenure at a stated interest rate. You typically know the maturity amount up front (for that rate and compounding), which makes FDs popular for short-to-medium goals and conservative sleeves — though interest is usually taxable and may lag long-run equity returns after inflation.

Strengths: Predictable rate for the tenure, simple product, optional premature break (often with penalty), senior-citizen and tax-saving variants at many banks.
Limits: Rate may not beat inflation after tax; early exit costs; NBFC/bank credit risk differs; DICGC insurance has a per-bank cap (see below).

Common FD types

Type Idea Often used for

Safety & DICGC (high level)

Deposits with insured banks are covered by the Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) up to a notified limit per bank per depositor (commonly discussed as ₹5 lakh including principal and interest — confirm current limit on official RBI/DICGC sources). Coverage does not make every NBFC deposit equivalent to a bank FD.

  • Prefer regulated banks/NBFCs you understand
  • Don’t put more than the insured limit in one bank if insurance is your safety model
  • Read premature-withdrawal and auto-renewal terms

Tax notes on FD interest (high level)

Not tax advice. Rules, TDS thresholds, and forms change. Confirm with the Income Tax Department or a qualified professional.
  • Interest is generally taxable under the head applicable to you (often as income from other sources).
  • Banks may deduct TDS when interest exceeds notified thresholds (figures change — verify for the year).
  • Form 15G/15H is discussed when total income is below taxable limits — eligibility is personal.
  • Tax-saving FDs and Section 80C interactions depend on regime and law for that year — see Tax overview and PPF & tax-saving.

Common mistakes

  • Locking emergency money in long FDs without a break plan
  • Ignoring tax and comparing pre-tax FD rates to equity returns
  • Auto-renewing forever without matching goals
  • Chasing the highest NBFC rate without understanding risk and insurance

Practical tips

  • Ladder maturities so cash becomes available every year or quarter.
  • Prefer cumulative FDs if you do not need periodic interest payouts.
  • Keep some money liquid for true emergencies — Emergency fund guide.
  • For long-horizon growth, pair FDs with diversified equity/funds — Risk & allocation.

FAQs

  • Can I break an FD early? Usually yes, often with a penalty and reduced rate — check your bank.
  • Is interest taxable? Generally yes, as per your status and current law.
  • Cumulative vs non-cumulative? Cumulative compounds to maturity; non-cumulative pays interest periodically.
  • FD vs debt fund? FD rate is contractual for the deposit; debt fund NAV can move with markets.
