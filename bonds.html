About Bonds

Bonds are fixed income instruments that represent a loan made by an investor to a government or corporation. They pay regular interest and return the principal at maturity, offering a more predictable income stream than stocks. Bonds play a crucial role in diversifying an investment portfolio and managing risk.

Benefits of Investing in Bonds

  • Stable Income: Regular interest payouts provide predictable cash flow.
  • Lower Risk: Bonds are generally less volatile than stocks.
  • Diversification: Help balance portfolio risk and reduce dependence on equities.
  • Capital Preservation: Return of principal at maturity ensures safety of capital.
  • Tax Benefits: Some bonds, like tax-free government bonds, offer tax incentives.

How to Invest in Bonds

  • Open a Demat account with brokers like Groww, Zerodha, or ICICI Direct.
  • Purchase government bonds, RBI Retail Direct, tax-free bonds, or listed corporate bonds.
  • Consider bond mutual funds or debt funds for a diversified bond portfolio.
  • Invest in primary auctions (for government bonds) or secondary market (NSE/BSE).
  • Look at platforms like RBI Retail Direct for direct access to government securities.
