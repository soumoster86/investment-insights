Risk & asset allocation

How to think about ups and downs, diversify without overcomplicating, and match investments to time horizons. Educational framework only — not a personal portfolio plan.

What “risk” means for investors

In everyday talk, risk means “I might lose money.” In investing, risk also means uncertainty of returns and the chance that money is not available when you need it (liquidity), or does not keep up with inflation (purchasing-power risk).

Rule of thumb: Money needed in the next 1–3 years usually belongs in safer, more stable options. Longer horizons can often tolerate more market volatility — if you can stay invested.

Common types of risk

  • Market risk: Prices of stocks, equity funds, and many bonds move with markets and news.
  • Inflation risk: Very “safe” low returns may lag rising costs of living.
  • Credit risk: A bond issuer may delay or fail to pay interest/principal.
  • Liquidity risk: You cannot exit quickly without a poor price (some products, small stocks).
  • Concentration risk: Too much in one stock, sector, or theme.
  • Behavioural risk: Panic selling, FOMO buying, abandoning SIPs at the worst time.

Asset allocation in plain language

Asset allocation is how you split money across broad buckets such as:

  • Equity — stocks, equity mutual funds, equity ETFs (growth potential, higher swings)
  • Debt / fixed income — FDs, debt funds, PPF, many bonds (stability focus, still not zero risk)
  • Cash / liquid — savings, liquid funds (emergency and near-term needs)
  • Other — gold, REITs, international equities, crypto (each with its own risks)

Allocation usually matters more over time than picking the single “best” fund every year.

Illustrative mixes (not advice)

These sketches show how time horizon can influence thinking. Your situation may differ.

Near-term goal (0–3 years)

  • Lower equity share
  • Higher debt / FD / liquid
  • Prioritise capital stability

Medium goal (3–7 years)

  • Balanced equity + debt
  • SIPs can still suit equity portion
  • Review as the goal date nears

Long-term (7+ years / retirement)

  • Higher growth assets possible
  • Need stomach for drawdowns
  • NPS / EPF / PPF may sit in the plan

Equity-like Debt / fixed Cash / liquid

Not personalised: Percentages above are teaching examples only. Age, income stability, dependents, and goals all change what is appropriate.

Rebalancing

Markets move. A portfolio that started 60% equity may become 75% equity after a rally — or shrink after a fall. Rebalancing means occasionally nudging weights back toward your intended mix (by directing new SIPs, or carefully shifting amounts).

Many people review once or twice a year rather than constantly tinkering.

Behaviour traps that feel like “strategy”

  • Recency bias: Assuming last year’s winners will keep winning.
  • Loss aversion: Selling everything after a crash and missing recoveries.
  • FOMO: Chasing tips, IPOs, or crypto spikes without a plan.
  • Overconfidence: Concentrating in one stock or theme you “know.”
Process beats prediction: Written goals, automatic SIPs, and a simple allocation reduce the need to guess the market every week.
