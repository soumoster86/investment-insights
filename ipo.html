What is an IPO?

IPO stands for Initial Public Offering. It is the process through which a private company offers its shares to the public for the first time and becomes a publicly listed company on a stock exchange (like NSE or BSE in India). IPOs are major milestones in a company's growth and offer investors an early opportunity to participate in a company’s potential.

How Does an IPO Work?

  1. The company files a draft red herring prospectus (DRHP) with SEBI detailing its business, finances, and reasons for raising funds.
  2. SEBI reviews and approves the IPO.
  3. The company sets a price band and IPO dates.
  4. Investors apply for shares during the IPO window (typically 3–5 days).
  5. Shares are allotted based on demand and listing takes place on the stock exchange.

Why Invest in IPOs?

  • Early Access: Invest in a company before it starts trading on the stock exchange.
  • Listing Gains: Some IPOs offer immediate gains if the listing price is higher than the IPO price.
  • Long-Term Growth: Opportunity to invest in promising businesses early in their public journey.
  • Portfolio Diversification: New companies can offer sector or business diversity.

Risks of IPO Investing

  • Overvaluation: Some IPOs may be aggressively priced.
  • Volatility: Prices can fluctuate sharply on listing and in the first few weeks.
  • Allotment Risk: High demand can mean partial or no allotment for retail investors.
  • Uncertain Track Record: Less historical data compared to established listed companies.

How to Apply for an IPO in India

  1. Open a Demat and trading account with a SEBI-registered broker (Zerodha, Groww, Upstox, ICICI, etc.).
  2. Login to your broker’s app/platform during the IPO window.
  3. Select the IPO, enter bid details (quantity, price), and provide your UPI ID for payment.
  4. Approve the payment request in your UPI app.
  5. Wait for the allotment result (announced a few days after IPO closes).
  6. Allotted shares are credited to your Demat account on listing day.

FAQs about IPOs

What is the minimum amount needed to apply for an IPO?
Usually, the minimum bid is one lot (often 10–15 shares). The cost depends on the price band (e.g., ₹15,000–₹20,000 for most mainboard IPOs).
How are IPO shares allotted?
Allotment is via lottery if demand exceeds supply. If under-subscribed, all valid bids may get full allotment.
Can I sell IPO shares on listing day?
Yes, once shares are credited to your Demat, you can sell anytime during market hours.
Is IPO investment risky?
Yes. IPOs can be volatile and past performance is no guarantee of future results. Review the DRHP and company fundamentals before investing.
Are IPO gains taxable?
Yes. Gains are subject to capital gains tax (short-term if sold within 1 year, long-term after 1 year, as per equity rules).
